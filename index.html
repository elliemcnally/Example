Hazy Days: A Breathing Simulator

Introduction

Hazy Days, created by Mike Ren, is a powerful and innovative game designed to shed light on the challenges of living in heavily polluted environments. By taking control of Xiao Feng, a young girl navigating her daily routine in a smog-filled city, players gain firsthand experience of the difficulties in maintaining health amidst air pollution.

Visual Aesthetics

The game features a subdued yet evocative color palette, reflecting the haze and gloom of polluted urban settings. Characters and environments are presented in a charming, minimalist art style that effectively conveys the somber tone of the narrative.

Mini Games in Hazy Days

Breathing Control: This mini-game challenges players to manage Xiao Feng's breathing while walking through smog-filled streets. Players must time their breaths carefully to avoid inhaling too much polluted air, illustrating the difficulty of simply moving around in such an environment.

Home Activities: In this mini-game, Xiao Feng performs everyday tasks like studying or eating while managing her exposure to indoor pollution. The player must balance her focus and health, showing how pollution doesn't stop at the doorstep.

School Commute: This mini-game requires players to navigate Xiao Feng's journey to school. Along the way, players encounter obstacles like crowded buses and street vendors, each posing unique air quality challenges. This emphasizes the constant vigilance required to protect one's health in polluted areas.

Health Monitoring: This segment of the game tracks Xiao Feng's overall health. Players must make decisions about her activities and rest periods to ensure she remains as healthy as possible. It highlights the cumulative effects of pollution over time.
